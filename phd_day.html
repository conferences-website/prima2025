PhD Day

The PRIMA 2025 PhD Day offers a forum for doctoral students in multi-agent systems and related fields to present their research, exchange feedback, and build international connections. Submissions are invited from PhD candidates at any stage of their doctorate.

Topics of Interest

Multi-Agent Systems Game Theory Logic in AI Verification Learning in MAS Human–Agent Interaction Simulation Autonomy Coordination Trust Security & Privacy Applications in Science Applications in Industry Applications in Society

Who Should Submit

  • PhD students (any stage)
  • Recent graduates (defended in 2025) may submit work completed during their PhD.

Submission Format

  • Extended abstract, up to 4 pages including references (LNCS style)
  • Clearly state: problem, motivation, approach, preliminary/expected results, and open challenges
  • Optional one figure (architecture, workflow, result)
  • Submissions are single-author (the PhD student). You may acknowledge advisors/collaborators
  • Submit via email to the PhD Day Chairs:
    • James Ortiz
    • Marija Slavkovik
    Please copy both

Presentation

Accepted authors will give a short presentation during the PhD Day, which will take place on 19th December 2025.
Extended abstracts will be made available on the conference website as non-archival material.

Travel Support (AIXIA)

Thanks to AIXIA, a total of 500€ is available to support PhD student participation. The number and amount of scholarships will be determined by the organizers depending on the number of requests.
To be considered, please add a short Funding Request paragraph to your abstract (need, estimated budget, other support).

Participation Details
