Palazzo Molza

The Camera di Commercio di Modena is located in Palazzo Molza, a Renaissance palace later remodelled in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Known for its elegant neoclassical architecture, the building features frescoed halls and decorative elements attributed to Giuseppe Pelacani.

In 1859, Giuseppe Garibaldi stayed here during a crucial phase of the Italian unification, adding further historical significance to the venue.
