Discover Modena

Modena, located in the heart of the Emilia-Romagna region, is a city where historical heritage, scientific innovation, and academic excellence coexist seamlessly. Its vibrant centre, renowned for Romanesque architecture and cultural dynamism, is home to the University of Modena and Reggio Emilia (UNIMORE), founded in 1175. With its unique blend of tradition, innovation, and accessibility, Modena offers the perfect environment to host PRIMA 2025.

UNESCO Heritage

The UNESCO World Heritage site of Modena includes the Romanesque Cathedral, the Ghirlandina bell tower, and Piazza Grande with the historic Palazzo Comunale. Nearby, the Ducal Palace — now home to the Italian Military Academy —, the Teatro Comunale Pavarotti–Freni, and the Albinelli Market illustrate the city's architectural and civic evolution across the centuries.

Automotive City

Modena’s longstanding commitment to technical innovation is reflected in its automotive legacy. As the birthplace of Enzo Ferrari and home to Ferrari, Maserati, and Pagani, the city represents a unique intersection of mechanical engineering, design, and research excellence.

Taste of Modena

The region’s culinary tradition is world-renowned. Modena is the birthplace of traditional balsamic vinegar, a product protected by designation of origin. Local specialities include Parmigiano Reggiano, Prosciutto di Modena, Lambrusco wine, and fresh pasta varieties such as tortellini and tagliatelle, alongside the iconic gnocco fritto.

How to Reach Modena

Modena is located in northern Italy and is easily accessible by train, car, and air. Below are the main travel options and practical details to reach the city and the PRIMA 2025 venue.

By Train

Modena is well connected via high-speed and regional trains. It lies on the Milan–Bologna line and can be reached from Bologna Centrale in about 20 minutes.

Station: Modena Railway Station (Piazza Dante Alighieri).

Tickets: Available at the station, on Trenitalia.com, or via the Trenitalia app.

By Plane

The closest airport is Bologna Guglielmo Marconi (BLQ), about 40 km from Modena.

From the airport: Take the Marconi Express to Bologna Centrale, then a direct train to Modena.
Alternatively, taxis and shuttle services are available.

By Car

Modena is easily accessible via the A1 motorway (Autostrada del Sole).
Exits: Modena Nord or Modena Sud.

Recommended parking (outside ZTL):

  • Parking del Centro – Viale Achille Fontanelli, 18 (8 min walk)
  • Garage Ferrari – Viale Trento Trieste, 33 (20 min walk)
  • Ex-AMCM – Via Antonio Peretti, 9 (24 min walk)
  • Street parking (blue lines) is available around the historic centre; fees vary depending on proximity
The conference venue is located near Modena’s Limited Traffic Zone (ZTL), which restricts and monitors vehicle access to the historic city centre. Attendees are advised to park outside the ZTL and reach the venue on foot.
